# Trend & Momentum Skills

## Overview
Trend analysis identifies directional movements and momentum. Focus: short to medium-term (days to weeks). Distinguish between trending moves (continuation likely) and noisy consolidations (mean reversion likely).

## Defining Trends

### Uptrend
- **Definition**: Series of higher highs and higher lows over time (days/weeks/months)
- **Strength**: Steeper slope + fewer dips = stronger uptrend
- **Duration**: How long has trend persisted (1 week vs. 3 months)?
  - Longer trends = more established, higher probability of continuation
  - Very new trends (< 2 days) = fragile, prone to shakeouts
- **Break Signal**: Lower low (below previous low) = uptrend break. Sell or reduce.

### Downtrend
- **Definition**: Series of lower highs and lower lows
- **Strength**: Steeper slope + fewer bounces = stronger downtrend
- **Duration**: Same as uptrend (longer = more durable)
- **Break Signal**: Higher high (above previous high) = downtrend break. Cover shorts or step aside.

### Sideways / Consolidation
- **Definition**: Price oscillating in a range (support to resistance) for > 2 weeks
- **Behavior**: Neither higher highs/lows (uptrend) nor lower highs/lows (downtrend)
- **Signal**: Consolidation often precedes breakout in trend direction
  - Flag or pennant pattern = breakout likely
  - Rectangle (tight, horizontal range) = breakout unpredictable
- **Avoid**: Trading within consolidation ranges; wait for breakout.

## Momentum Signals

### Momentum Definition
- **Velocity**: How fast price is moving
- **Direction**: Up = bullish momentum; Down = bearish momentum

### High Momentum Indicators
- **Large Daily Moves** (> 2-3% in a day)
- **Gap Up/Down** (overnight jump): High conviction (news-driven) or low conviction (overnight holdings)
- **Volume Spike**: Large move on high volume = strong conviction. Large move on low volume = weak.
- **Trend Acceleration**: Price moving faster than trend line slope

### Momentum Exhaustion Signals
- **Decreasing Volume While Price Rallies**: Uptrend weakening (fewer buyers willing at higher prices)
- **RSI > 70 + Rising Above 70**: Peak momentum. Pullback likely soon.
- **Price Gap but Volume Declines**: Exhaustion gap (reversal coming) vs. runaway gap (continuation)
  - Runaway gap = volume surge + gap + resuming trend
  - Exhaustion gap = volume declines + gap + reversal within 3-5 days
- **Multiple Reversal Candles**: After strong rally, shooting stars or bearish engulfing = seller capitulation

## Breakout vs. Breakdown

### Breakout (Bullish)
- **Definition**: Price closes above resistance level on high volume
- **Confirmation**: Volume must exceed average (>120% of 20-day avg)
- **Probability**: ~65% of breakouts lead to continuation (per studies)
- **Target**: Often moves distance = (resistance - support) added to breakout point
- **Retest**: Common to retest broken resistance as new support within 1-5 days
  - Buy on retest = strong entry

### Breakdown (Bearish)
- **Definition**: Price closes below support level on high volume
- **Confirmation**: Volume must exceed average
- **Probability**: ~65% lead to continuation
- **Target**: Support level becomes resistance when broken; downside = (support - resistance) below break
- **Retest**: Cover shorts on retest of broken support; watch for reversal

### False Breakout (Trap)
- **Definition**: Price breaks level but closes back inside the level before day end
- **Red Flag**: Volume declining on breakout (weak conviction)
- **Pattern**: Common in choppy or low-liquidity environments
- **Avoid**: Don't chase breakouts on low volume or during low-liquidity sessions (pre-market, post-market)

## Momentum Divergence

### Bullish Divergence
- **Setup**: Price makes new low, but momentum indicator (RSI, MACD) doesn't make new low
- **Signal**: Weakening downside momentum = bounce likely
- **Strength**: More credible if divergence persists for 3+ price bars
- **Probability**: ~70% probability of bounce within 3-5 days

### Bearish Divergence
- **Setup**: Price makes new high, but momentum indicator doesn't make new high
- **Signal**: Weakening upside momentum = pullback likely
- **Strength**: More credible if divergence spans 3+ bars
- **Probability**: ~70% probability of pullback within 3-5 days

## Volatility & Trend Quality

### High Volatility (VIX > 25)
- **Trend Quality**: Trends weaker, consolidations more likely
- **Move Size**: Larger daily swings (easier to get stopped out)
- **Opportunity**: Large moves create more trade opportunities, but higher risk
- **Strategy**: Widen stops, reduce position size

### Low Volatility (VIX < 12)
- **Trend Quality**: Trends stronger, breakouts more reliable
- **Move Size**: Smaller daily swings (tight action)
- **Risk**: Complacency; sudden volatility spike can shock market
- **Setup**: Lowest volatility often precedes largest moves (calm before storm)

## Momentum Reversal Signals

### V-Bottom Reversal
- **Setup**: Strong downtrend suddenly reverses (often on volume surge)
- **Pattern**: Sharp down → gap down open → closes up on volume
- **Signal**: Capitulation (all sellers exhausted); buyers step in
- **Probability**: High probability upside follow-through for 3-5 days

### Climactic Top Reversal
- **Setup**: Parabolic rally followed by sell-off
- **Pattern**: Extremely high volume on top day → gap down next day
- **Signal**: Euphoria exhaustion; reversal likely
- **Probability**: High probability downside follow-through

## Trend Following Strategy

### Entry on Trend Confirmation
1. Wait for clear trend establishment (3+ higher highs/lows OR breakout)
2. Enter on pullback within trend (safer than breakout chase)
3. Place stop below recent swing low (uptrend) or above swing high (downtrend)
4. Target = support/resistance level ahead in trend

### Exit Rules
1. **Trend Break**: Close below trend line (downtrend) = sell
2. **Resistance**: Price reaches prior high = take profit or reduce
3. **Divergence**: Momentum divergence = reduce or exit
4. **Time Decay**: Trend > 4-6 weeks on single timeframe = reduce (fatigue)

## Scoring Guidelines

- **Strong Buy Trend**: Clear uptrend 2+ weeks, breakout on volume, RSI 40-60, bullish divergence
- **Buy Trend**: Uptrend forming, breakout likely, momentum positive, pullback within trend
- **Neutral**: Consolidation phase, no clear direction, mixed signals
- **Sell Trend**: Downtrend established 2+ weeks, breakdown on volume, RSI 40-60, bearish divergence
- **Strong Sell Trend**: Parabolic downtrend, capitulation, high volume breakdown, weakness accelerating

## Caveats

- Trends can reverse suddenly on news or macro shifts (don't be stubborn)
- Trending in one timeframe may be consolidation in larger timeframe
- Breakouts fail ~35% of the time (not reliable alone)
- Use trend + momentum + fundamentals for best conviction trades
