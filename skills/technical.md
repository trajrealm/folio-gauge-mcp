# Technical Analysis Skills

## Overview
Technical analysis uses price action, volume, and indicators to identify trends and entry/exit opportunities. This skill focuses on short to medium-term signals (days to weeks).

## Core Indicators

### Moving Averages (SMA, EMA)
- **SMA (Simple Moving Average)**: Unweighted average over N periods. Lagging but smooth.
  - SMA 50 > SMA 200: Bullish trend
  - SMA 20 crossover above SMA 50: Momentum reversal (bullish entry)
- **EMA (Exponential Moving Average)**: More weight on recent prices. Faster response to changes.
  - EMA 12 > EMA 26: Bullish momentum
  - EMA crossover often precedes SMA by 1-2 candles

### Relative Strength Index (RSI)
- Scale: 0-100
- **Overbought** (RSI > 70): Potential pullback or consolidation. Not always bearish in strong trends.
- **Oversold** (RSI < 30): Potential bounce. Watch for reversal candles.
- **Divergence**: Price makes new high but RSI doesn't → weakening momentum (bearish signal)

### MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
- Line = EMA12 - EMA26
- Signal = EMA9(Line)
- Histogram = Line - Signal
- **Bullish signals**:
  - Line > Signal (positive histogram, green)
  - Histogram crosses above zero
  - Line higher lows while price makes lower lows (bullish divergence)
- **Bearish signals**: Reverse of above

## Candlestick Patterns

### Trend Patterns
- **Higher Highs + Higher Lows**: Uptrend. Strong unless broken.
- **Lower Highs + Lower Lows**: Downtrend. Sell rallies into resistance.

### Reversal Patterns
- **Engulfing**: Previous candle fully contained in current candle. High conviction reversal.
- **Hammer** (after downtrend): Long lower wick, small body. Potential bounce.
- **Shooting Star** (after uptrend): Long upper wick, small body. Rejection of highs.
- **Doji**: Open ≈ Close. Indecision. Look for confirmation candle.

### Continuation Patterns
- **Flag**: Quick spike followed by consolidation in tight range. Breakout likely in trend direction.
- **Pennant**: Converging highs/lows within uptrend/downtrend. Continuation expected.

## Support & Resistance

### Identification
- **Support**: Price level where buying pressure stops decline. Often previous lows, round numbers, psychological levels.
- **Resistance**: Price level where selling pressure stops advance. Often previous highs.
- **Confirmation**: A level becomes stronger support/resistance if tested 2-3 times without breaking.

### Trading Rules
- **Bounce at Support**: Bullish setup if price holds support + reversal candle forms.
- **Break of Resistance**: Bullish breakout if volume increases. Retest common: wait for retest to support for confirmation entry.
- **False Breakout**: Price breaks level but closes back inside range. Trap. Avoid chasing.

## Volume Analysis

### High Volume Signals
- **On Up Days**: Bullish. Confirms uptrend continuation.
- **On Down Days**: Bearish. Confirms downtrend.
- **On Low Volume**: Weak move. Likely to reverse.

### Volume + Price Action
- **Climactic Volume**: Extremely high volume on large move. Often marks exhaustion → reversal.
- **Drying Up**: Volume declining while price stalls. Caution on trend continuation.

## Scoring Guidelines

- **Strong Buy**: EMA bullish, RSI 30-50 (room to run), price above SMA 20/50, breaks resistance on volume
- **Buy**: EMA bullish OR price above SMA 200, RSI not overbought
- **Hold/Neutral**: No clear trend, conflicting signals, consolidation phase
- **Sell**: EMA bearish, price below SMA 20/50, RSI overbought, breaks support on volume
- **Strong Sell**: EMA bearish, RSI 50-70, price below SMA 200, breaks support on climactic volume

## Risk Management

- **Stop Loss**: Below recent swing low on long entry. Guard against rapid reversals.
- **Take Profit**: At resistance level or when RSI reaches 70+. Lock in gains.
- **Position Sizing**: Scale into trend. Don't load entire position at entry.
- **Drawdown Limit**: If stock drops >10% below entry, reassess thesis.
