# Stock Discovery & Screening Skills

## Overview
Discovery is about finding new investment opportunities using systematic screening, pattern recognition, and heuristics. Focus: new ideas and candidates. Once screened, refer to other analysts for deeper evaluation (technical, fundamental, sentiment, etc.).

## Screening Heuristics

### Value Screening
- **Criteria**:
  - P/E < 15 and < peer average
  - P/B < 1.0
  - Dividend yield > 3%
  - Debt/Equity < 1.0
- **Rationale**: Undervalued relative to book value and peers
- **Caution**: Many value traps (cheap for good reason). Combine with fundamental strength.

### Growth Screening
- **Criteria**:
  - Revenue growth > 20% YoY
  - EPS growth > 15% YoY
  - PEG < 1.5
  - Profit margin > industry median
- **Rationale**: Growing faster than peers at reasonable valuation
- **Caution**: High expectations (earnings misses = sharp sell-offs)

### Dividend Aristocrats
- **Criteria**:
  - Dividend yield 2-5%
  - Dividend growth > 5% CAGR past 10 years
  - Payout ratio < 60% (room to grow dividend)
  - Debt stable or declining
- **Rationale**: Reliable and growing income stream (defensive)
- **Use**: Stable portfolio core; lower volatility

### Breakout Screening
- **Criteria**:
  - Price closes above 52-week high on volume > 150% of average
  - Relative strength (14-week % move) > peer median
  - RSI 50-70 (momentum without overbought)
- **Rationale**: Technical breakout often leads to continuation (3-5 week trend)
- **Use**: Short to medium-term momentum play

### Reversal Screening
- **Criteria**:
  - Price near 52-week low (< 70% of 52-week high)
  - Relative strength turning positive (last 2 weeks better than prior 10 weeks)
  - RSI 20-40 (oversold but recovering)
  - Bullish divergence (price low but MACD/RSI higher than previous low)
- **Rationale**: Despair exhaustion; recovery likely
- **Use**: Medium-term bounce play

## Trend-Following Discovery

### Trend Identification
1. **Multi-Timeframe Strength**: Stock strong on daily AND weekly charts (not just daily spike)
   - Easier screening: 50-day SMA > 200-day SMA (uptrend)
2. **Sector Momentum**: Stock's sector outperforming market
   - Stock often continues if sector has momentum (rising tide)
3. **Relative Strength**: Stock outperforming market (higher % move)
   - Stock > SPY performance over 3-4 weeks = leadership

### Example Screen
- Daily: Price > 50-day SMA > 200-day SMA
- Weekly: 50-week RSI > 50 (uptrend)
- Sector: Sector outperforming SPY past 4 weeks
- Relative: Stock > SPY performance past 4 weeks
- Volume: Volume expansion on recent rally

## Contrarian Discovery

### Oversold Setup
- **Criteria**:
  - Price down 30-50% from recent highs
  - RSI < 30
  - Volume spike on down day (panic selling)
  - No fundamental deterioration (check earnings, debt, guidance)
- **Rationale**: Panic selling = opportunity; rebound likely
- **Caution**: Sometimes capitulation is justified. Research reason for crash.

### Unpopular Sector
- **Criteria**:
  - Sector P/E < market average by > 30%
  - Sector outflows (fund redemptions)
  - Sector technical weak but macro/fundamental backdrop improving
- **Rationale**: Sector ignored by market; rotation into sector likely
- **Example**: Energy beaten down despite rising oil prices

## Catalyst-Based Discovery

### Upcoming Catalysts
- **Earnings Announcements**: Upcoming earnings often lead to vol expansion + movement
- **FDA Approvals**: Biotech stocks often pre-announce decision date (binary event)
- **Earnings Revisions**: Analyst estimate cuts/raises; track unusual revisions (leading indicator)
- **Index Inclusion**: Stock added to major index = automatic inflows
- **Dividend Announcements**: New dividend often rallies yield-seeking investors
- **Earnings Surprises**: Historical: beats often lead to multi-day drift higher

### Pre-Catalyst Positioning
- **High IV**: Before earnings, implied vol rises. Caution on buying calls (expensive premium).
- **Post-Catalyst**: Often better entry after initial move (digestion phase).

## Relative Value Discovery

### Peer Comparison Screening
- **Undervalued vs. Peers**:
  - Target P/E 20% below peer median
  - Target PEG 15% below peer median
  - Target margin > peer median (quality)
  - Target growth > peer median (justified lower P/E)
- **Rationale**: Relative value play (mean reversion to peer valuation)

### Sector Relative Strength
- **Cheap Sector + Rising Sector Performance**:
  - Energy P/E 40% below market, but XLE ratio > 1.0 past month
  - Likely sector rotation into energy; pick sector leaders
- **Rationale**: Sector leadership + valuation = upside potential

## Earnings Quality Discovery

### Quality Screening
- **Criteria**:
  - Operating cash flow > net income (earnings backed by cash)
  - Free cash flow > 0 (can fund growth or buybacks)
  - Revenue growing faster than receivables (not extending credit to boost sales)
  - Inventory stable (not building up unsold goods)
- **Rationale**: Earnings are "real", not accounting-managed
- **Use**: Combine with valuation for higher-conviction plays

### Earnings Surprise Screening
- **Criteria**:
  - Historical: Beat estimates > 50% of quarters (consistent beater)
  - Last quarter: Beat by > 5%
  - Analyst estimates trend: Rising revisions past 3 months
- **Rationale**: Earnings surprises drive stock outperformance
- **Use**: Add to existing position or build new one

## Red Flags to Avoid

### Avoid Traps
- **Insider Selling**: CEO/board selling heavily (loss of confidence)
- **Deteriorating Margins**: Even if revenue growing, compression = pressure (cost inflation or price war)
- **Rising Debt**: Especially if revenue flat or declining (financial stress)
- **Negative Free Cash Flow**: Can't sustain for long; eventually cuts dividend or raises capital
- **High Valuation on Slowing Growth**: P/E 40x but growth declining = multiple compression risk

### Fundamental Red Flags
- Accounting restatements (earnings quality issue)
- Major customer loss (revenue concentration risk)
- Regulatory investigation (legal/PR risk)
- Key executive departures (management quality concern)
- Dividend cut (loss of confidence, cash flow pressure)

## Screening Workflow

1. **Run Screens**: Apply 1-3 heuristics based on macro environment (growth screen in expansion, value screen in contraction)
2. **Rank Results**: Score candidates by:
   - Valuation (cheap vs. peers, vs. sector)
   - Quality (margin trends, cash flow, debt)
   - Catalyst (upcoming earnings, product launch)
3. **Research**: Dive into top 3-5 candidates
   - Read recent earnings call
   - Check analyst consensus
   - Scan news for red flags
4. **Refer to Specialists**: Pass winner to technical, fundamental, sentiment analysts for final thesis

## Scoring Guidelines

- **High Conviction Screen Hit**: Multiple positive signals (breakout + valuation + fundamentals), no red flags, clear catalyst
- **Medium Conviction**: 1-2 positive signals, no major red flags, worth deeper research
- **Low Conviction**: Single signal or conflicting signals, research required before considering
- **Avoid (Red Flags)**: Earnings quality issues, insider selling, deteriorating fundamentals, regulatory risks
