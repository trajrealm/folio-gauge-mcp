# Macro Economics Skills

## Overview
Macro analysis examines economy-wide conditions affecting all equities: interest rates, inflation, employment, GDP growth. Focus: medium to long-term (weeks to months). Macro drives sector rotations and risk appetite.

## Federal Reserve & Interest Rates

### Fed Policy Stance
- **Hawkish** (Raising Rates): Combat inflation. Headwind for equities (higher discount rate, lower valuations).
  - Higher debt service costs for corporates
  - Savers favor bonds over stocks
  - Growth stocks (high P/E) hurt most; value stocks (dividend yielders) hurt less
- **Dovish** (Cutting Rates): Support economy/growth. Tailwind for equities (lower discount rate, higher valuations).
  - Cost of capital falls; growth projects attractive
  - Credit expansion; easier financing
  - Risk-on sentiment; outflows from bonds to stocks

### Federal Funds Rate (FFR)
- The rate banks lend to each other overnight. Fed controls via open market operations.
- **Current Rate vs. Neutral Rate**: If FFR > neutral (e.g., 5.5% vs. 3.5% estimated), rates restrictive.
  - Restrictive = slowing growth; watch for recession signals
- **Forward Guidance**: Fed's stated path for next 6-12 months.
  - If Fed signals "higher for longer" = headwind for growth equities
  - If Fed signals "cuts coming" = tailwind for equities

### Yield Curve
- **Inversion** (Long-term yields < Short-term yields): Recession warning. Market pricing end to rate hikes + economic slowdown.
  - Historical: Inversion precedes recession by 6-18 months
  - After inversion persists 3-6 months, recession probability high
- **Normal** (Long yields > Short yields): Economic growth expectation. Bullish for equities.
- **Steep**: Strong growth + inflation expectations. Sector rotation to cyclicals.
- **Flat**: Transition period. High uncertainty.

## Inflation & Deflation

### CPI (Consumer Price Index)
- **High Inflation** (CPI > 3-4% YoY):
  - Fed likely tightening (rate hikes)
  - Corporate margins compress (can't fully pass costs to consumers)
  - Bonds become attractive; stocks underperform
  - Commodities rally
- **Low Inflation** (CPI < 2%):
  - Fed likely easing (rate cuts)
  - Real wages improve; consumer spending grows
  - Growth stocks attract investors
  - Bond yields fall; valuations expand

### Deflation Risk (CPI < 0%):
- Recession signal. Companies cut prices; demand weakens. Death spiral if not arrested quickly.
- Government stimulus + rate cuts deployed aggressively (Japan, 2000s)

### Core CPI vs. Headline CPI
- **Headline**: Includes volatile commodities (oil, food). Noisy signal.
- **Core**: Excludes commodities. Smoother, more predictive of trend inflation.
- **Watch**: If core > headline for 2+ months, underlying inflation pressure rising (hawkish).

## Employment & Wages

### Unemployment Rate
- **Low Unemployment** (< 4%): Tight labor market. Wages rising. Companies struggle to hire.
  - Inflation pressure (wage-price spiral risk)
  - Fed may hold rates higher or tighten further
- **Rising Unemployment** (> 5-6%): Labor slack. Wage pressure easing. Recession risk if rising rapidly.
  - Fed pivot to cuts likely
  - Growth stocks outperform

### Job Creation / Nonfarm Payroll
- **Strong** (> 200K added/month): Economy healthy. Growth narrative intact.
- **Weak** (< 100K): Slowdown warning. Companies cutting headcount.
- **Rapidly Declining**: Recession indicator.

### Wage Growth (Average Hourly Earnings)
- **Rapid wage growth + low unemployment**: Inflation spiral risk (Fed tightens).
- **Flat wage growth + rising unemployment**: Disinflationary; recession risk (Fed eases).

## GDP & Growth

### Real GDP Growth
- **Positive Growth** (> 2% annualized): Economy expanding. Equity-friendly.
- **Slowing Growth** (0-2%): Deceleration. Recession risk if continues to negative.
- **Negative Growth** (< 0%, 2 consecutive quarters): Recession. High equity drawdown risk.
- **Watch**: Leading indicators (PMI, consumer confidence) often signal GDP shift 1-3 months ahead.

### PMI (Purchasing Managers' Index)
- **> 50**: Expansion. Manufacturing/services growing.
- **< 50**: Contraction. Slowdown ahead.
- **> 55 sustained**: Very strong. Risk of overheating (inflation).
- **< 45 sustained**: Severe slowdown. Recession likely.

## Risk On / Risk Off

### VIX (Volatility Index)
- **VIX < 15**: Low volatility, complacency. Risk-on appetite. Growth stocks outperform.
- **VIX 15-20**: Normal. Balanced risk/reward.
- **VIX > 25**: High fear. Risk-off mode. Defensive stocks and bonds outperform.
- **VIX > 40**: Panic. Capitulation often near. Opportunity for contrarian long.

### Credit Spreads (High Yield vs. Treasury)
- **Narrow Spreads**: Confidence in corporate credit. Risk-on. Equities attractive.
- **Widening Spreads**: Credit stress. Recession fear. Flight to safety (bonds outperform equities).
- **Rapid Widening**: Market dislocation. Liquidity crisis risk (2008, 2020 analogs).

## Sector Rotation by Macro Regime

### Early Cycle (Accelerating Growth, Low Inflation)
- Outperformers: Cyclicals (industrials, materials, consumer discretionary), Tech, Growth
- Underperformers: Utilities, Staples

### Mid Cycle (High Growth, Rising Inflation)
- Outperformers: Financials (wider spreads), Energy, Materials
- Underperformers: Long-duration bonds, Utilities (fixed dividend cut by inflation)

### Late Cycle (Slowing Growth, High Inflation)
- Outperformers: Healthcare, Staples (defensive), Utilities
- Underperformers: Cyclicals, growth at risk of earnings cuts

### Recession (Negative Growth, Falling Inflation)
- Outperformers: Treasuries, Staples, Healthcare, Utilities
- Underperformers: Cyclicals, energy, high-beta tech

## Scoring Guidelines

- **Strong Buy Environment**: Real rates negative, Fed cutting, inflation under control, unemployment stable, PMI > 52, VIX < 12
- **Buy Environment**: Positive GDP growth, Fed on pause, inflation moderate, employment stable, PMI 50-55, VIX 12-18
- **Neutral**: Mixed signals, transition period, late-cycle indicators
- **Sell Environment**: Growth slowing, Fed tightening, inflation rising or deflation risk, unemployment rising, PMI < 50, VIX > 20
- **Strong Sell Environment**: Recession probable, Fed defensive, financial stress, credit spreads widening rapidly, VIX > 30

## Key Macro Indicators to Monitor
1. Fed funds rate & forward guidance
2. Yield curve (10Y - 2Y spread)
3. CPI (especially core) month-over-month and YoY
4. Unemployment rate
5. Nonfarm payroll jobs
6. Real GDP growth (quarterly)
7. PMI (manufacturing and services)
8. VIX and credit spreads
9. Housing starts & mortgage rates
10. Consumer confidence & retail sales
