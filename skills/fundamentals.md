# Fundamentals Analysis Skills

## Overview
Fundamental analysis examines a company's financial health, profitability, growth, and valuation. Focus: medium to long-term (weeks to months). Ignore noise; focus on earnings quality and revenue growth.

## Valuation Metrics

### Price-to-Earnings Ratio (P/E)
- **Formula**: Stock Price / Earnings Per Share (EPS)
- **Interpretation**:
  - P/E < 10: Cheap (value play) or distressed. Research why.
  - P/E 10-20: Fair value. Typical for mature companies.
  - P/E > 30: Growth premium. Expect 15%+ annual earnings growth to justify.
  - P/E < 0: Unprofitable. Higher risk.
- **Traps**: High P/E on temporary earnings decline. Cyclical trough earnings inflate P/E.

### Forward P/E
- Uses next 12 months earnings guidance, not trailing.
- More relevant for growth expectations.
- Lower than trailing P/E = analysts expect earnings growth
- Higher than trailing P/E = earnings decline expected

### PEG Ratio (P/E to Growth)
- **Formula**: P/E / Expected Annual Earnings Growth Rate (%)
- **Rule of Thumb**: PEG < 1.0 = undervalued growth stock. PEG > 2.0 = overvalued.
- **Advantage**: Balances valuation against growth expectations.

### Price-to-Book Ratio (P/B)
- **Formula**: Stock Price / Book Value Per Share
- **Use**: Asset-heavy sectors (banks, manufacturing, utilities)
- **P/B < 1.0**: Potential value if assets are productive
- **P/B > 3.0**: Premium valuation or intangible value (brand, IP)

## Profitability Metrics

### Profit Margin (Net Margin)
- **Formula**: Net Income / Revenue
- **Benchmarks**:
  - Software/SaaS: 15-30%+ (high margin)
  - Retail: 2-5% (thin margin)
  - Banks: 20-30% (depends on risk)
- **Trend**: Rising margin = improving efficiency. Falling margin = pressure (competition or costs).

### Return on Equity (ROE)
- **Formula**: Net Income / Shareholders' Equity
- **Benchmark**: 15%+ is excellent. < 8% is weak.
- **Interpretation**: How efficiently company converts shareholder capital into profit.

### Return on Assets (ROA)
- **Formula**: Net Income / Total Assets
- **Benchmark**: 5%+ is good. Shows asset productivity.

## Growth Metrics

### Revenue Growth
- **Benchmark**: 10-20% annual growth = healthy. > 30% = strong growth. < 5% = mature or declining.
- **Trend**: Accelerating growth → bullish. Decelerating growth → caution.
- **Guidance**: Compare actual vs. management guidance. Beats = credibility. Misses = red flag.

### Earnings Per Share (EPS) Growth
- More important than revenue growth (profitability drives stock price).
- **Year-over-Year (YoY) Growth**: 10%+ = solid. > 20% = strong.
- **Consistency**: Predictable growth beats volatile growth.

### Earnings Surprise
- **Positive Surprise** (beat): Stock often rallies in days following earnings.
- **Negative Surprise** (miss): Sharp sell-off. Reassess thesis.
- **Forward Guidance**: Management's outlook for next quarter/year. Miss on guidance = bigger red flag than earnings miss.

## Debt & Solvency

### Debt-to-Equity Ratio
- **Formula**: Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity
- **Benchmark**: < 1.0 = conservative. 1-2.0 = moderate. > 3.0 = risky.
- **Industry Context**: Banks naturally have high leverage; tech has low leverage.

### Interest Coverage Ratio
- **Formula**: EBIT / Interest Expense
- **Benchmark**: > 5.0 = safe. 2-5 = moderate. < 2 = risky (may struggle to pay interest).
- **Watch**: Declining ratio = margin pressure or rising debt.

### Current Ratio
- **Formula**: Current Assets / Current Liabilities
- **Benchmark**: 1.5-2.0 = healthy liquidity. < 1.0 = potential distress.

## Quality of Earnings

### Cash Flow vs. Earnings
- **Operating Cash Flow > Net Income**: Good. Earnings are "real" (backed by cash).
- **Operating Cash Flow < Net Income**: Caution. Earnings may be inflated by accounting tricks.
- **Free Cash Flow**: Operating Cash Flow - CapEx. > 0 = company can fund dividends/buybacks/debt payoff.

### Accounting Red Flags
- Rising earnings but declining cash flow
- Increasing accounts receivable (customers not paying)
- Increasing inventory (not selling products)
- Large one-time items or write-downs

## Earnings Calendar

### Before Earnings
- **Volatility Expansion**: Options become expensive. IV crush on earnings.
- **Positioning**: Analyst previews, whisper numbers, positioning ahead of announcement.

### Earnings Announcement
- **Magnitude**: Large beat/miss can trigger 5-10%+ move.
- **Guidance**: Often more important than reported earnings.
- **Conference Call**: Management tone, forward outlook, competitive positioning.

### Post-Earnings
- **Drift**: Prices often drift over days/weeks following earnings (not all digested at open).
- **Revisit Thesis**: Update valuation based on new guidance. Is stock still attractive?

## Scoring Guidelines

- **Strong Buy**: Revenue growing > 15%, EPS growth > 15%, margins expanding, valuation reasonable (PEG < 1.2), positive earnings surprise
- **Buy**: Revenue growing 8-15%, stable/growing margins, P/E < sector average, FCF positive
- **Hold/Neutral**: Mature business, stable earnings, fair valuation, no catalyst
- **Sell**: Revenue declining or stalling, margins compressing, negative FCF, valuation excessive, earnings guidance down
- **Strong Sell**: Negative earnings surprise, guidance withdrawn, debt rising, cash burn accelerating

## Key Research Sources
- Company 10-Q (quarterly) and 10-K (annual) filings
- Earnings call transcripts
- Analyst consensus estimates (not individual opinions)
- Industry peers' metrics (for relative assessment)
